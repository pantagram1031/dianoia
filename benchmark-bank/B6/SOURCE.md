## Problem Statement

Let `H` be a finite graph, and call a countably infinite graph `G` strongly
locally `H`-induced-saturated if `G` is `H`-free and every nonempty locally
finite perturbation of `G` contains an induced copy of `H`.

1. For which finite graphs `H` does the 2026 Bonamy-Groenland-Johnston-Morrison-Scott theorem guarantee the existence of a strongly locally `H`-induced-saturated countably infinite graph? State the exact excluded cases.
2. Does the same theorem prove that for every finite non-clique, non-independent-set `H` there exists a finite `H`-induced-saturated graph? Answer yes or no, and justify.
3. Does the paper prove the analogous locally finite perturbation statement for nontransitive finite tournaments or for nontrivial finite `k`-uniform hypergraphs? Answer yes/no for each and distinguish theorem from conjecture.
