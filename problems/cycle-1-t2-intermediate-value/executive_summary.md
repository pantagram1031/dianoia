The intermediate value theorem is proved by the standard completeness argument, with the classical status stated explicitly. After reducing to f(a) <= 0 <= f(b), endpoint-zero cases close immediately. Otherwise set S = {x in [a,b] : f(x) <= 0} and let c be its supremum. Continuity at c prevents f(c) from being negative, since then points just to the right would stay in S, and prevents f(c) from being positive, since points of S approach c from the left. Hence f(c)=0.
