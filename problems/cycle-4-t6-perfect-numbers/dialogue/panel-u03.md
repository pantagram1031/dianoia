# Panel u03

Question: Which direct alternative should be pursued after the first obstruction: infinitude or finiteness?

number-theorist: Infinitude reduces to proving infinitely many Mersenne primes through the Euclid-Euler characterization of even perfect numbers. That is not currently available.

combinatorialist: Finiteness would need a counting obstruction that rules out infinitely many Mersenne prime exponents and also handles the odd-perfect-number branch.

analyst: Density heuristics do not decide existence or finiteness, and should not be converted into proof.

Disagreement: number-theorist initially explored infinitude; combinatorialist pushed the finiteness route. Both routes hit recognized frontier barriers.

Resolution: Record FAILURE-AMBITION-GAP with both direct alternatives attempted and killed; do not promote a theorem.
