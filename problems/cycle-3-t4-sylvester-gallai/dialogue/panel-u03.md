# Panel u03

Question: Should the direct attack use combinatorial induction or the minimal-distance geometry proof?

geometer: Use the minimum positive distance from a point P in S to a line L determined by two other points of S. This attacks the original finite noncollinear target directly.

combinatorialist: Induction risks losing the exact ordinary-line conclusion. The rich-line case should be falsified by adjacent points on L around the foot of the perpendicular.

analyst: The finite set of candidate nonincident point-line distances has a positive minimum, so the optimization step is legitimate without compactness.

Disagreement: combinatorialist initially preferred induction, but accepted the minimal-distance route after the finite-minimum invariant was stated.

Resolution: Use the minimal-distance proof; falsifiability hook is whether every rich-line minimum produces a strictly closer nonincident pair.
