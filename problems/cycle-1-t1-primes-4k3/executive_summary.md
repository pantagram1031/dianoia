The target theorem is proved directly. Given a finite alleged list of all primes congruent to 3 modulo 4 up to a bound, multiply them to get Q and form M = 4Q - 1. A prime divisor of M must include at least one prime congruent to 3 modulo 4, since M itself is congruent to 3 modulo 4. That divisor is not in the original list because every listed prime divides Q but not M. This contradiction yields a new prime of the required congruence class beyond the bound.
