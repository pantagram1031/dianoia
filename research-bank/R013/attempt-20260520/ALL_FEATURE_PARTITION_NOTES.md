## Interpretation

Most unprocessed buckets live in feature classes that never appear among the
near-boundary processed buckets. That is good for a local-degree proof: many
classes can plausibly be discharged by a generic "extra density gives slack"
argument.

The two mixed groups are more important. They show that:

- cover count, adjacent-edge count, skip-edge count, and the current density
  flags are insufficient to isolate the extremal mechanisms;
- at least one finer feature is needed, probably distinguishing which skip
  cover lands in the top rank and whether a bottom skip is redundant with an
  adjacent path;
- the next proof-support step should refine the feature key, not immediately
  hand-count another bucket.

## Next Feature Candidates

Add one or more of:

- exact skip vector: `(r0_to_r2, r0_to_r3, r1_to_r3)`;
- adjacent vector: `(r0_to_r1, r1_to_r2, r2_to_r3)`;
- whether every rank-1 element has an upper cover into rank 2;
- whether every rank-2 element has a lower cover from rank 1;
- whether a skip cover is transitively shadowed by adjacent covers through
  another element.

The first two are already present in `features`; the command should optionally
include them in the grouping key so mixed groups can be separated without
manual inspection.
