## Interpretation

This refutes the simplest possible dominance story. The proof target cannot be:

```text
Every unprocessed class has componentwise more adjacent/skip slack than a
processed near-boundary class.
```

The frontier class U6 is the opposite: it has fewer skip covers than all
processed classes, yet its minimum lower probability is safely `3/7`.

The better proof shape is two-stage:

1. Prove the no-skip frontier vector `(2,2,1;0,0,0)` directly.
2. Classify skip additions from that frontier. Some skip additions are
   dangerous and land in processed P1-P8; all other additions appear to leave
   enough slack to stay above `13/32`.

This is a mathematical improvement over the previous table because it replaces
51 unprocessed vector classes with one frontier plus a skip-placement
classification problem.
