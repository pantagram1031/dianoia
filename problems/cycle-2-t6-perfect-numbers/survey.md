# Survey

## State of the Art
1. [C-001] Euclid, c. 300 BCE, Elements, Book IX Proposition 36: if 2^p-1 is prime, then 2^(p-1)(2^p-1) is perfect.
2. [C-002] Euler, 1849, Opera postuma I, theorem on even perfect numbers: every even perfect number has Euclid's form.
3. [C-003] Nielsen, 2012, Odd perfect numbers have at least nine distinct prime factors, Mathematics of Computation 81 Theorem 1.1: lower bound on distinct prime factors of odd perfect numbers.

## Open Terrain Map
- [FRONTIER] Infinitude of Mersenne primes is open, so Euclid's construction does not prove infinitely many perfect numbers.
- [FRONTIER] Existence of odd perfect numbers is open, so finiteness cannot be forced by current classification.
